The problem

A blank page is the slowest part.

Writing a product landing page means staring at an empty screen and making a hundred small decisions — what to lead with, which benefits matter, how to structure it, what tone to strike. Most AI page tools hand you a generic template and ask you to fill it in, which just moves the blank page around. I wanted something that did the actual thinking: read the product, decide the angle, and produce a page worth editing instead of a skeleton worth abandoning.

How it works

One input, a full page out.

From the person's side it's a single step: paste a product URL. Behind that, Pagecraft does three things in sequence — and the design challenge was making something that complex feel like one calm action.

01

Reads the product

It pulls and interprets the real product — what it is, who it's for, what makes it worth buying.

02

Decides the angle

It chooses a positioning and structure rather than dropping content into a fixed template.

03

Generates the page

It produces a full, conversion-ready landing page — hero, benefits, structure — in one pass.

From raw URL to page

What "decides the angle" actually means.

The difference between a template filler and Pagecraft is what happens between input and output. Instead of mapping fields, it interprets the product and commits to a way of selling it.

Input
A product URL — nothing more. No brief, no copy, no structure provided.
Output
A complete page with a chosen angle and structure
Designing the system behind it

The real design work was invisible.

The interface is deliberately minimal — the hard design happened in the layer most people never see: the skills and prompts that decide how the AI reads a product and structures a page. I authored custom design-thinking skills so the output reflects real positioning logic, not generic filler.

Layer 01

The interface

One URL field and a clear result. Everything kept out of the way so the product does the talking.

Layer 02

The skills

Custom design-thinking skills that encode how to position a product and structure a page that converts.

Layer 03

The generation

Claude + Lovable turning that logic into a real, editable page — reliably, in one pass.

The point of view

Most AI tools hand you a template to fill in. Pagecraft makes the decision for you — and gives you something worth editing.

That's the bet: the value isn't generating words, it's making the structural and positioning decisions that usually stall people before they start.
