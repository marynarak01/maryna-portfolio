The challenge

Two products that have to agree.

A marketplace is really two products wearing one coat. Sellers need a tool that makes listing fast and forgiving — variants, pricing, inventory, the messy reality of a catalogue. Customers need a storefront that's clean, trustworthy, and easy to shop. The hard part is that both sides read from the same product data: if the CMS lets a seller enter something the storefront can't show cleanly, the whole thing breaks down.

My job was to design both halves so they stayed consistent — so what a seller types in one place shows up correctly, and predictably, in the place a customer buys it.

Seller CMS

Product creation, broken into calm pieces.

Instead of one enormous product form, I designed the CMS as modular sections — each handling one part of a listing. A seller builds a product step by step, and every field maps to something the storefront knows how to display.

Module 01

Listings

Core product details — title, description, media — structured so the storefront renders them consistently.

Module 02

Pricing

Price, compare‑at, and currency handling, kept in one predictable place.

Module 03

Inventory

Stock tracking per item, so availability on the storefront reflects reality.

Module 04

Variants

Size, colour, and option combinations — the part most likely to get messy, made structured.

Module 05

Organisation

Categories and collections that drive how products are grouped and found.

Module 06

Media

Image handling designed so the storefront always has what it needs to look right.

Storefront

The same data, made shoppable.

The storefront was designed against the CMS's data structures — so the product page a customer sees is a direct, clean expression of what the seller entered. No mismatches, no fields that exist in one place but not the other.

Keeping them in sync

One source of truth, two experiences.

The core design principle: seller inputs and customer‑facing pages are two views of one product model. Designing them together — not as separate projects — is what keeps them consistent.
