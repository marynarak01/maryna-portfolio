What the client needed

Set it up, change the look, and keep it running.

The store

Reworking the standard theme.

The store started from a default Shopify theme. The homepage, collection pages, and product pages were reworked to give it its own look, while keeping the theme stable and easy for the client to use.

The product page

Most of the work was here.

Shopify's standard product template doesn't have many sections, so the product page took the most work. A set of custom fields and sections was built so each product page could hold more — features, a full description, specs, an FAQ — and so the same layout could be reused across products and the different templates the client needed.

Title, price, variants

The top of the page — title, reviews, price, discount, and variant options.

Key features

A custom section for the main features and benefits.

Full description

Custom blocks for a longer, structured description.

Specs & package

Collapsible sections for sizes, materials, and what's in the box.

FAQ

A custom field set for common questions on the page.

Trust & payment badges

Security and payment icons near the buy button.

These were built as reusable custom fields, so the same layout could be applied across products and adapted into the different product templates.

Mobile

Most of the store's traffic is on mobile, so the pages were checked on the phone as well.

Ongoing support

Looking after the store afterwards.

After the build, the store was handled day to day, working from what the marketer and the data needed. When the marketing team was busy, that also included help with content.

◆Adding new products and setting up their pages from the templates.
◆Editing product images and adding the product information.
◆Writing and adjusting product descriptions.
◆Making Instagram and Facebook content when the team needed it.
